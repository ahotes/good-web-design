Good vs. Bad Web Design

Websites have changed astronomically as the internet has been around, and over that span of time, the principles of web design have drastically changed. What was passible website design back in the mid 90's and early 2000's is, at this point, functionally burdensome and visually antiquated.

I researched web design principles from two sites I found on the internet, and based on what I found, here are some websites that exemplify either good or bad design principles.

Good web design...

GitHub

Link to homepage

I would consider GitHub's website design good based on its content presentation, it clearly communicates its purpose, and it flows well. All of these things are standards for good web design.

Mozilla Developer Network

Link to homepage

I consider Mozilla's Developer Network a well designed site because it clearly communicates its purpose, its well organized, flows well, and is easily navigated.

Bad web design...

Ling's Cars

Link to homepage

Ling's Cars is a satirical website where the owner of the company "Lings Cars" is using the site to advertise and sell cars of different types. The site itself though is designed to be comedically flashy, chaotic, and a general eyesore to look at. Too many animations and pictures moving all the time, as well as other excessive features are present all over the site at all times.

CraigsList

Link to homepage

CraigsList is a general advertising forum for a variety of products and services, generally within a certain geographic region. I would consider CraigsList to be an example of bad web desgin (or at least antiquated), because its presentation doesn't make its function immediately apparent, and isn't very user friendly!
